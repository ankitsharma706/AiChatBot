# Afterma Platform — Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## What is Afterma?
Afterma is a maternal health technology platform designed to support Indian mothers through pregnancy, childbirth, and postpartum recovery. It provides AI-powered health guidance, symptom tracking, doctor consultations, medication reminders, and access to government welfare schemes.

## Who can use Afterma?
Afterma is designed for:
- Expectant mothers (all trimesters of pregnancy)
- New mothers in postpartum recovery (normal and C-section deliveries)
- Mothers experiencing postpartum mental health challenges
- Families supporting a mother through recovery

## Is Afterma a substitute for a doctor?
No. Afterma provides general health guidance and information only. It is NOT a medical service and does NOT replace a qualified doctor. Always consult a registered medical professional for specific diagnoses or treatment decisions.

## What languages does Afterma support?
Afterma supports English and Hindi. Our AI assistant will respond in the same language as your question.

## How does Afterma protect my health data?
Afterma uses industry-standard encryption (AES-256) for all health data. Your data is never sold to third parties. You can delete your account and all associated data at any time.

## Postpartum Recovery FAQs

### How long does postpartum recovery take?
Recovery time varies:
- **Normal delivery**: Most mothers feel physically recovered in 4–6 weeks. Emotional recovery may take 3–6 months.
- **C-section delivery**: Physical recovery typically takes 6–8 weeks for the incision site. Core strength may take 3–6 months to restore.

### What are normal postpartum symptoms?
Common and expected postpartum symptoms include:
- Vaginal discharge (lochia) — bright red for the first week, then pink/brown, then yellowish
- Uterine cramps (afterpains) — especially during breastfeeding
- Breast engorgement and tenderness
- Mood swings, tearfulness (baby blues) in the first 2 weeks
- Fatigue and sleep disruption
- Perineal soreness (after normal delivery)
- Incision pain and numbness (after C-section)

### When should I see a doctor after delivery?
Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:
- Heavy bleeding (soaking more than 1 pad per hour for 2+ hours)
- Fever above 100.4°F (38°C)
- Signs of infection at incision/perineum (redness, pus, foul smell)
- Difficulty breathing or chest pain
- Thoughts of harming yourself or your baby
- Severe headache or vision changes

### What is baby blues vs. Postpartum Depression (PPD)?
**Baby Blues**: Normal emotional fluctuations in the first 1–2 weeks postpartum. Characterized by crying, mood swings, anxiety, and irritability. Resolves on its own with rest and support.

**Postpartum Depression (PPD)**: A clinical condition lasting more than 2 weeks with symptoms including persistent sadness, inability to care for baby, withdrawal, hopelessness, and in severe cases, thoughts of self-harm. PPD requires professional treatment — speak with a doctor immediately.

## Breastfeeding FAQs

### How often should I breastfeed?
Newborns should be fed on demand, typically every 1.5–3 hours, or 8–12 times in 24 hours. Watch for early hunger cues: rooting, sucking on hands, turning head.

### Why does breastfeeding hurt?
Initial discomfort (first 10–15 seconds of each feed) is common in the first week. Persistent, sharp pain usually indicates a latch issue. Consult a lactation consultant for help with positioning. Cracked or bleeding nipples require medical evaluation.

### How do I know if my baby is getting enough milk?
Signs of adequate feeding:
- 6–8 wet diapers per day after Day 4
- Regular bowel movements (yellow, seedy stools in breastfed newborns)
- Weight gain (baby should regain birth weight by 10–14 days)
- Baby seems satisfied after feeds

### What is mastitis?
Mastitis is a breast infection causing redness, warmth, swelling, fever, and flu-like symptoms. It requires antibiotic treatment — do not delay seeing a doctor. You can usually continue breastfeeding.

## Nutrition FAQs

### What foods are best for postpartum recovery in India?
Recommended foods for postpartum recovery:
- **Iron-rich foods**: methi (fenugreek) seeds, palak (spinach), rajma, liver, eggs, jaggery (gur)
- **Protein sources**: dal, paneer, eggs, chicken, fish, milk
- **Calcium**: milk, curd, ragi, sesame seeds (til)
- **Healthy fats**: ghee (in moderation), nuts, flaxseeds
- **Hydration**: coconut water, daal ka paani, milk, water (minimum 2.5–3 litres/day)

Traditional Indian foods like ajwain (carom) ladoo, gondh (edible gum) ladoo, methi laddoo, and haldi doodh (turmeric milk) are beneficial for recovery and are culturally validated.

### Can I eat spicy food while breastfeeding?
Most spicy foods are safe during breastfeeding. Babies are rarely affected by maternal diet. However, if you notice your baby becomes fussy or gassy after you eat certain foods, try eliminating them for a few days.

### Do I need iron supplements after delivery?
Iron supplementation is commonly recommended after delivery, especially if you had significant blood loss. Your doctor will advise the appropriate dosage. Signs of iron deficiency include excessive fatigue, pale skin, and breathlessness.
