# Postpartum Recovery Guidelines — Afterma Clinical Reference

## Physical Recovery — Normal Delivery

### Week 1 (Days 1–7)
- **Lochia (vaginal discharge)**: Red/dark red discharge is normal. Should decrease by Day 5.
  - ⚠ Warning: Soaking more than 1 pad per hour = emergency (hemorrhage)
- **Perineal discomfort**: Soreness, swelling — use ice packs, sitz baths (warm water soaking)
- **Afterpains/cramps**: Uterus contracting back — stronger with breastfeeding; normal for 3-5 days
- **Breast engorgement**: Days 3-4; warm compress before feeds, cold compress after
- **Rest**: Prioritize sleep whenever baby sleeps

### Week 2–6
- Gradual increase in activity — short walks first
- Avoid heavy lifting (>5 kg)
- Kegel exercises can begin around Week 2
- Scar care (episiotomy): keep clean, air-dry after bathing

### 6-Week Checkup
- Blood pressure, weights, wound inspection
- Contraception discussion
- Mental health screening (EPDS)
- Return to sex discussion (typically after 6 weeks + clearance)

## Physical Recovery — C-Section (Caesarean)

### First Week
- Pain IS normal — take prescribed pain relief
- Gas and bloating very common — walk short distances to help
- Shoulder/chest pain = referred gas pain (normal)
- Wound: keep dry, no submerging for 4–6 weeks

### Week 2–4
- Walking is encouraged — increases circulation, prevents clots (DVT)
- Avoid stairs if possible in first 2 weeks
- No driving for 4–6 weeks
- No sex, swimming, baths for 6 weeks

### Red Flags (C-section wound):
- Redness spreading beyond wound edges
- Yellow/green discharge or foul smell
- Opening of wound (dehiscence)
- Fever >38°C (100.4°F)
- Increasing (not decreasing) pain after Day 5

## Postpartum Nutrition — Indian Context

### Essential Nutrients
| Nutrient | Recommendation | Indian Sources |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| Iron | 27 mg/day (breastfeeding) | Spinach (palak), rajma, jaggery (gud), ragi |
| Calcium | 1000 mg/day | Milk, curd (dahi), sesame (til), ragi |
| Protein | 65–70 g/day | Dal, paneer, eggs, chicken, soya |
| Omega-3 | 300 mg DHA | Fish, flaxseeds (alsi), walnuts (akhrot) |
| Vitamin D | 600 IU/day | Sunlight, eggs, fortified milk |

### Traditional Indian Postpartum Foods (evidence-supported)
- **Ajwain (carom seeds) water**: Aids digestion, reduces gas, uterine recovery
- **Methi (fenugreek) ladoo**: Galactagogue — supports milk production
- **Gond ke ladoo**: Dink ladoos — joint support, energy
- **Haldi (turmeric) milk**: Anti-inflammatory, wound healing support
- **Lauki (bottle gourd) sabzi**: Easy to digest, hydrating

### Foods to Limit (first 4 weeks)
- Raw salads, cold foods (traditional practice — warming foods preferred)
- Excessive spicy food if breastfeeding and baby shows colic
- Gas-producing vegetables (excess cabbage, raw beans)

## Postpartum Mental Health

### Baby Blues (Normal)
- Affects 70–80% of mothers
- Days 1–2 post-delivery onwards, peaks Day 3–5
- Duration: up to 2 weeks
- Symptoms: tearfulness, mood swings, irritability, anxiety
- Management: rest, support, reassurance

### Postpartum Depression (PPD) — Needs Medical Help
- Affects ~10–15% of mothers (likely underreported in India)
- Onset: anytime in first year, most common in first 3 months
- Symptoms:
  - Persistent sadness > 2 weeks
  - Inability to care for baby or self
  - Intrusive thoughts (seek help immediately)
  - Severe anxiety, panic attacks
  - Feeling disconnected from baby (not bonding)
- Screening: Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS) — score ≥13 requires physician review

### Postpartum Anxiety
- Often underdiagnosed; can occur alongside or without depression
- Symptoms: racing heart, excessive worry about baby's health, inability to sleep even when baby sleeps
- Management: therapy (CBT), lifestyle, medication if severe (consult psychiatrist)

## Lactation — Breastfeeding Support

### Getting Started
- First hour after delivery: skin-to-skin, initiate breastfeeding
- Colostrum (first milk) — small amounts, yellow/golden — very nutritious
- Mature milk comes in Day 3–5

### Common Issues
**Low Milk Supply**
- Most perceived low supply is not actually low
- Ensure baby is feeding 8–12 times/day
- Adequate hydration (3L/day)
- Galactagogues: methi, saboodana (in moderation), oats

**Mastitis**
- Symptoms: hard, red, painful breast + fever ≥38.5°C
- Action: CONTINUE breastfeeding from affected side, warm compress, rest
- ⚠ Requires antibiotic — consult doctor within 24 hours
- Untreated can progress to abscess (emergency)

**Cracked/Sore Nipples**
- Check latch first (wide open mouth, lips flanged out)
- Apply own breast milk after feeds — natural antiseptic
- Lanolin cream safe for breastfeeding mothers

## Exercise Progressions — Postpartum

### Phase 1 (Week 1–2): Breathing & Minimal Movement
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Foot circles and ankle pumps (DVT prevention)
- Gentle pelvic floor activation (if no pain)

### Phase 2 (Week 2–6): Gentle Mobility
- Short walks (5–10 minutes, increasing gradually)
- Gentle stretching
- Continued pelvic floor exercises (Kegels)
- Avoid crunches, situps, planks — risk of diastasis recti

### Phase 3 (6 Weeks+, with doctor clearance): Return to Fitness
- Walking 20–30 minutes
- Yoga (postpartum specific)
- Swimming (after wound fully healed)
- Cycling (light)
- Avoid high-impact until pelvic floor is strong

## Medication Safety — Postpartum & Breastfeeding

### Generally Safe (Commonly Prescribed)
- Paracetamol (Crocin, Dolo): Safe for pain, fever
- Ibuprofen (Brufen): Safe short-term while breastfeeding
- Lactulose: Safe stool softener
- Iron supplements: Prescribed postpartum, may cause constipation
- Vitamin D supplements: Safe and recommended

### Avoid Without Doctor Guidance
- Aspirin (avoid in breastfeeding)
- Codeine-containing medicines
- Any herbal supplements without physician clearance

## Emergency Signs — Seek Immediate Help (112)

| Symptom | Possible Cause |
|---------|---------------|
| Soaking >1 pad/hour | Postpartum hemorrhage |
| Chest pain + shortness of breath | Pulmonary embolism / heart |
| Severe headache + visual changes | Postpartum preeclampsia |
| High fever >38.5°C + chills | Infection / sepsis |
| Calf swelling + pain (one leg) | Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) |
| Suicidal thoughts | Mental health emergency |
| Seizures | Eclampsia |
| Wound opening with pus | Dehiscence / infection |
