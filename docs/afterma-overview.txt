AFTERMA PLATFORM OVERVIEW
==========================
Afterma is a postpartum and maternal recovery platform designed for Indian mothers.
It provides evidence-based, culturally sensitive guidance for recovery after childbirth.

CORE MISSION:
Support mothers through postpartum recovery, pregnancy, and pre-conception phases
with AI-powered guidance, triage, and community support.

KEY HEALTH DOMAINS:
1. Physical Recovery — wound healing, C-section care, pelvic floor rehabilitation
2. Mental Wellness — postpartum depression screening (EPDS), mood tracking, PPD support
3. Lactation — breastfeeding guidance, latch support, mastitis identification
4. Nutrition — Indian postpartum diet, calcium, iron, protein needs
5. Exercise — gentle return-to-fitness protocols for new mothers
6. Medication Adherence — iron supplements, vitamin D, prenatal vitamins
7. Baby Care — newborn health markers, feeding, sleep patterns

TRIAGE FRAMEWORK:
- MILD: Normal recovery symptoms — offer self-care, reassurance, home remedies
- MODERATE: Symptoms needing monitoring — advise doctor consultation within 24-48 hours
- EMERGENCY: Life-threatening signs — direct to ER/112 immediately

SAFETY RULES:
- Never diagnose specific conditions
- Never prescribe or recommend specific doses of medications
- Always recommend doctor consultation for persistent symptoms
- Indian emergency number: 112
- Postpartum mental health helpline: iCall 9152987821
- NIMHANS: 080-46110007

INDIAN HEALTHCARE CONTEXT:
- Most Indian mothers deliver in government or private hospitals
- Majority return home within 2-4 days of delivery
- Family support (mother/mother-in-law) plays primary caregiving role
- Common traditional practices: oil massage (malish), restricted diet for 40 days (jaapa)
- Privacy concerns — many mothers hesitant to discuss symptoms openly
- Language: respond in Hindi or English based on user preference

POSTPARTUM TIMELINE (NORMAL DELIVERY):
- Days 1-7: Close monitoring, lochia (discharge) expected, breast engorgement
- Week 2-6: Gradual mobility, avoid heavy lifting, pelvic rest
- 6 weeks: Standard OB checkup, return to normal activity discussion
- 3-6 months: Full physical recovery for most women

C-SECTION SPECIFIC:
- Wound care: keep dry, watch for redness/discharge
- No driving for 4-6 weeks
- No lifting > 4 kg for 6 weeks
- Bloating, gas, shoulder pain common in first week
- Longer recovery: 8-12 weeks for full healing
